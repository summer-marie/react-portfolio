# Visual Language

## Design Philosophy

The portfolio should feel like a modern software product rather than a traditional developer portfolio.

The experience should combine editorial clarity with subtle moments of delight through color, lighting, and motion.

The design should remain timeless and avoid following short-lived UI trends.

---

# Visual Keywords

* Quietly Premium
* Modern
* Editorial
* Atmospheric
* Intentional
* Approachable
* Polished
* Technical

---

# Color Philosophy

Color should create atmosphere rather than demand attention.

Use soft, muted tones with restrained gradients and lighting effects.

Avoid pure white and harsh black whenever possible.

### Principles

* Warm neutral backgrounds
* Muted accent colors
* Low saturation
* High contrast for readability
* Gradients only when emphasizing important content

---

# Typography

Typography is the primary visual element.

### Heading Style

* Large
* Confident
* Spacious
* Editorial

Suggested Font:

* Geist

### Body Style

* Highly readable
* Comfortable line height
* Minimal font weights

Suggested Font:

* Geist

### Monospace

Used sparingly.

Suggested Font:

* Geist Mono

---

# Spacing

Whitespace is part of the design.

Prioritize generous spacing over fitting more content.

Guidelines:

* Large page margins
* Consistent vertical rhythm
* Comfortable reading width
* Sections should feel independent

---

# Corners

Soft but modern.

Target radius:

* Cards: 16px
* Buttons: 12px
* Inputs: 10px

---

# Borders

Subtle.

Use borders primarily for separation rather than decoration.

Avoid heavy outlines.

---

# Shadows

Soft and layered.

Shadows should communicate depth without drawing attention.

Hover states may slightly increase elevation.

---

# Gradients

Use gradients sparingly.

Ideal locations:

* Hero background
* Section transitions
* Highlight cards
* Hover lighting
* Decorative ambient effects

Avoid rainbow or high-saturation gradients.

---

# Icons

Simple.

Consistent stroke width.

Prefer outline icons over filled icons.

---

# Imagery

Projects provide the primary visual interest.

Screenshots should be:

* Clean
* Large
* High quality
* Framed consistently

---

# Overall Experience

The portfolio should feel calm on first impression, then become increasingly interesting as users explore through subtle interactions and thoughtful details.
